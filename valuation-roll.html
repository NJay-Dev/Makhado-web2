VALUATION ROLL

The Makhado Local Municipality provides access to the General Valuation Roll and Supplementary Valuation Rolls for transparency and public awareness. The Valuation Roll is a public document that lists all properties within the municipal boundaries, their market values, categories, and other relevant information.

Property owners and stakeholders are encouraged to review these documents to ensure the accuracy of property valuations, which form the basis for property rates calculations. If you believe your property has been incorrectly valued, you may lodge an objection within the specified period using the appropriate objection forms provided below.

About the Valuation Roll

The Municipal Property Rates Act (Act 6 of 2004) requires municipalities to prepare a General Valuation Roll of all properties within their boundaries at least once every five years. The Valuation Roll is used to determine property rates, which are a significant source of revenue for the municipality.

General Valuation Roll: This is the main valuation roll that contains all properties within the municipal area and their market values as determined by professional valuers.

Supplementary Valuation Roll: These are updates to the General Valuation Roll that account for changes such as new developments, subdivisions, consolidations, and changes in property use or zoning.

Objection Process: Property owners who disagree with the valuation of their property can lodge an objection within the specified period (usually 30 days from the date of notice). Different objection forms are provided for different property types:

  • Form A: For residential properties
  • Form B: For properties other than residential or agricultural
  • Form C: For agricultural holdings or farms
