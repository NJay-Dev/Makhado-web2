AGRICULTURE IN MAKHADO

Agriculture is a vital sector in the Makhado Local Municipality, contributing significantly to the local economy and providing livelihoods for many residents. Our region is known for its fertile soil, favorable climate, and diverse agricultural activities.

The municipality supports agricultural development through various initiatives, programs, and partnerships with stakeholders in the agricultural sector.

Major Agricultural Products

Fruit Production

Makhado is known for its subtropical fruit production, including mangoes, avocados, bananas, litchis, and macadamia nuts.

Crop Farming

Various crops are grown in the region, including maize, vegetables, and other staple foods that support local food security.

Livestock Farming

Livestock farming is an important agricultural activity in Makhado, with cattle, goats, and poultry being the main livestock raised in the area.

Agricultural Initiatives

Smallholder Farmer Support

The municipality provides support to smallholder farmers through training programs, access to markets, and agricultural inputs to enhance productivity and sustainability.

Commercial Agriculture Development

We work with commercial farmers to promote sustainable agricultural practices, enhance productivity, and create employment opportunities in the agricultural sector.

Agricultural Projects

Community Gardens

Supporting community garden initiatives to enhance food security and promote sustainable agriculture at the household level.

Irrigation Schemes

Development and maintenance of irrigation schemes to support agricultural activities and enhance crop production.

Agricultural Training

Providing training and capacity building for farmers to enhance their skills and knowledge in modern agricultural practices.

Market Access

Facilitating market access for local farmers to sell their produce and connect with potential buyers and markets.
