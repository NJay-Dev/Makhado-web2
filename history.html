Origins of the name "Makhado"

Makhado was a 19th century VhaVenda king, King Makhado led the Venda people in withstanding attacks by encroaching Boers until he died in 1897.

1897

Establishment of Louis Trichardt

The Boers established the town of Louis Trichardt after defeating the Venda, but the Anglo-Boer War, which took place between 1899 and 1902, prevented full-scale Boer occupation as troops were withdrawn to fight the British.

1899

Renaming of Louis Trichardt to Makhado

In 2003, the town of Louis Trichardt was renamed Makhado to honor King Makhado's legacy, as part of a broader effort to recognize indigenous leaders and rectify historical injustices.

2003

Makhado as a Dynamic Municipality

Today, Makhado is a dynamic municipality known for its cultural heritage and economic activities, including agriculture and tourism. The town continues to evolve, reflecting the resilience and history of its people.

2025
