About Municipal Tariffs

Municipal tariffs are the fees charged by the municipality for various services provided to residents and businesses. These tariffs are reviewed and updated annually as part of the municipal budget process in accordance with the Municipal Finance Management Act (MFMA).

Types of Municipal Tariffs:

  • Property Rates: Taxes levied on the value of property within the municipal area.
  • Utility Services: Charges for electricity, water, sanitation, and refuse removal.
  • Community Facilities: Fees for the use of municipal facilities such as swimming pools, libraries, halls, stadiums, and caravan parks.
  • Administrative Services: Fees for services such as building plan approvals, town planning applications, and information services.
  • Business Licenses: Fees for various business licenses including hawkers, street trading, and inflammable liquids.

Tariff Policy: The municipality's tariff policy outlines the principles and guidelines for determining tariffs, including cost recovery, subsidization for indigent households, and the promotion of efficient resource use.

For any queries regarding municipal tariffs, please contact the Finance Department at the Makhado Local Municipality.
