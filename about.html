ABOUT OUR MUNICIPALITY

Makhado Municipality is strategically positioned in the northern region of Limpopo Province, approximately 100km from the Zimbabwean border along the N1 Route. Our municipality spans an impressive 831,058 hectares and serves as a vital gateway between South Africa and the rest of the African continent.

With a population of approximately 416,728 residents spread across our vast territory, Makhado is characterized by its predominantly rural landscape. Our geographic coordinates (23° 00´ 00´´ S 29° 45´ 00´´ E) place us in a unique position to serve as a cultural and economic hub for the region.

Demographic Information

The total population of Makhado has decreased from 516,031 in 2011 (based on the 2011 census) to 416,728 due to the new demarcation of the municipality boundaries. Similarly, the number of households has decreased from 134,889 (Census 2011) to 116,371, with approximately 145,147 registered voters.

Our municipality comprises 4 formal towns: Louis Trichardt, Vleifontein, Waterval, and Dzanani, along with more than 200 villages spread throughout the region. The main administrative office is located in Louis Trichardt town, supported by three regional administrative offices in Dzanani, Vleifontein, and Waterval.

Community Engagement

The Municipality has established 38 fully functional ward committees to ensure effective community participation and representation in municipal affairs.

These committees serve as vital links between the municipality and the communities it serves, facilitating two-way communication and ensuring that community needs and concerns are addressed in municipal planning and decision-making processes.
